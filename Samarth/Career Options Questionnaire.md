## Section 5 — Questions to Discuss with Samarth

The following questions should be asked directly to Samarth to refine the career recommendation:

1. **Interest in the human body:** When you think about biology or health science, do you find it genuinely interesting — or is it something you're doing because it's expected?

2. **Hands-on vs. thinking:** Do you prefer doing things with your hands (building, fixing, creating) or do you prefer analysing and solving problems mentally?

3. **People tolerance:** Do you enjoy being around people all day, or does it drain you? Are you comfortable with patients who are anxious or in pain?

4. **Blood/procedures:** Are you okay with the idea of working with blood, teeth, surgical procedures? Any strong aversions?

5. **Long study tolerance:** Can you commit to 8–10 years of training (dentistry) or 11–14 years (medicine)? What's the longest you've stuck with something you didn't immediately love?

6. **Autonomy vs. team:** Do you prefer working independently (own practice) or as part of a team (hospital)?

7. **Tech interest depth:** Is computing just something you're good at, or do you actually love building things / solving tech problems?

8. **Income vs. lifestyle:** Which matters more to you — high income or having control of your time and schedule?

9. **India comfort:** If the India BDS route (cheapest) were on the table, would you be comfortable living in India for 5 years, away from Canada/family?

10. **Pressure tolerance:** How do you perform under high-stakes, time-pressured situations (exams, emergencies)? Do you freeze or thrive?
